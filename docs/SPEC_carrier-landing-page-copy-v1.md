## 1. Purpose

This document specifies the copy and structure for two carrier-facing landing pages on CDLA.jobs:

- `cdla.jobs/partners/integration` — pitches Tier 2 (free) with Tenstreet integration as the included quality upgrade
- `cdla.jobs/partners/exclusivity` — pitches Tier 1 ($2,500/month flat, 24-hour exclusivity, priority placement)

Both pages share a common structure but pitch different commercial models to different audiences within carrier organizations. Drivers do not see these pages.

---

## 2. Strategic context

These pages are sales surfaces, not conversion surfaces. The carrier audience — recruiting directors, hiring managers, fleet owner-operators — makes B2B buying decisions deliberately. They are not impulse buyers. The page's job is to deliver enough credible information that the carrier is willing to take a sales call. The sales call closes the deal; the page qualifies the prospect.

CDLA.jobs is in beta at launch. No partner counts, hire numbers, or case studies are publishable yet. Trust signals on these pages rely on product-truth claims, pricing transparency, and the credibility of the prequalification model itself — the page is honest about what CDLA.jobs does and doesn't do, which is more credible than inflated claims to a recruiting director who has been pitched lead vendors for a decade.

Both pages are explicit: **CDLA.jobs is the contracting party** for Tier 1 and Tier 2 carrier subscriptions. PHTP does not appear on either page.

Brand voice on both pages: carrier-facing voice per Brand Voice Guide §4 — professional, credible, specific. No sarcasm, no buzzwords, prices visible early.

---

## 3. Shared page structure

Both pages render top to bottom in this order:

1. Hero (headline + subhead + primary CTA + price visible)
2. The model (what we do, what you do)
3. What's included (specific feature list for the tier)
4. Pricing transparency (exact numbers, what's not charged)
5. How it works (3-4 step onboarding)
6. FAQ (5-8 questions specific to the tier)
7. Secondary CTA + footer

Both pages are mobile-responsive but desktop-primary. Carriers research vendors on laptops, not phones. The chatbox-centric mobile-first design of the homepage does not apply here.

### 4.2 Section — The model

**Section header:**
> What CDLA.jobs does. What your team does.

**Two-column layout (stacks on mobile):**

**CDLA.jobs handles:**
- Driver intake (one form per driver, not 20 applications)
- Matching drivers to your hiring criteria

**Body copy:**

The prequalification model is intentional. CDLA.jobs is not a recruiter and not a background-check vendor. We are a matching service. Drivers tell us what they're looking for; carriers tell us what they're hiring for; we connect the two. Everything that touches FCRA, DOT 391, or the actual employment decision stays inside your hiring process where it belongs.

### 4.5 Section — How it works

**Section header:**
> How onboarding goes.

Four-step list:

**Step 1 — Hiring criteria call (30 minutes).**
We capture what you're hiring for. Equipment, regions, minimum experience, endorsement requirements, what you'll consider on safety history. Most carriers complete this in one call.

**Step 2 — Tenstreet integration setup.**
We work with your Tenstreet account team to configure the lead delivery integration. Typically completed within one business week. Your team's lift here is minimal — mostly approvals.

**Step 3 — Test lead.**
We send a synthetic test lead through the integration to confirm everything routes correctly to your Tenstreet pipeline.

**Step 4 — You're live.**
Matched driver prequalifications start flowing to your Tenstreet as they're generated. Most carriers see their first real match within days of going live.

### 4.6 FAQ

**Section header:**
> Common questions.

Each Q/A is short. 1-3 sentences per answer.

**Is this actually free, or is there a catch?**
It's actually free. Tier 2 has no monthly cost, no setup cost, no per-lead cost, and no per-hire cost. We make money on Tier 1 subscriptions from carriers who want priority placement and 24-hour exclusivity. Tier 2 is funded by the platform overall.

**How is this different from a lead vendor?**
Lead vendors sell the same driver's contact information to multiple carriers. CDLA.jobs drivers consent to share their prequalification with you specifically — not to a panel of buyers. The driver picks which carriers see them. We're a matching service, not a contact-list reseller.

**Do you run background checks?**
No. Background checks, MVR pulls, and FCRA-authorized record retrieval stay inside your existing application process in Tenstreet. We don't touch that — by design.

**What does the prequalification record include?**
What the driver disclosed on intake: experience, equipment, endorsements, schedule preference, regions, plus their stage 2 safety answers (accidents, violations, DUI, felony — as disclosed by the driver). The driver attests to accuracy. Carriers verify in their own application process.

**What if a driver disclosed something the carrier wouldn't accept?**
The matching engine filters drivers against your stated hiring criteria before any prequalification reaches you. If you don't accept failed DOT tests, drivers who failed a DOT test don't see you in their match results. You only see drivers whose disclosed history is within your stated tolerances.

**Can we change our hiring criteria later?**
Yes. Your hiring criteria are stored in our system and editable. Most carriers refine criteria in their first 30 days as they see what's coming through. Material changes (new equipment, new regions) can be picked up the same day.

**How do we cancel?**
You email us and we stop sending leads. No paperwork, no exit interview, no notice period. Tier 2 is built to make leaving as easy as starting.

**Do we have to use Tenstreet?**
The free integration is for Tenstreet specifically. Carriers on other ATS platforms (IntelliApp, DriverReach, etc.) receive matched leads by email. Tier 2 works for any carrier; the Tenstreet integration is a quality upgrade for carriers already on Tenstreet.

### 5.2 Section — The model

**Section header:**
> What Tier 1 gets you.

**Body copy:**

Tier 1 is the same matching engine as Tier 2, with three commercial differences:

**Exclusivity window.** When a driver matches your criteria, you see them first. For 24 hours, no other carrier sees that prequalification — even if the driver matches other carriers in their list. You decide whether to pursue. After 24 hours, the driver becomes visible to Tier 2 carriers as well.

**Priority placement.** When a driver views their match list, Tier 1 carriers appear first. The order within Tier 1 is determined by match-fit score against the driver's stated preferences.

**Quarterly business review.** Your account gets a quarterly call to review match volume, conversion data, and any criteria refinements. Tier 2 carriers get reporting; Tier 1 carriers get a working session with someone who knows their account.

Everything else — Tenstreet integration, prequalification model, no per-hire fees, you handle FCRA/391 — is identical to Tier 2.

### 5.3 Section — What's included

**Section header:**

Bulleted list:

- Free Tenstreet integration (setup and configuration handled by us)

- Match volume + conversion reporting
- Quarterly business review (60-minute account call)

### 5.4 Section — Pricing transparency

**Section header:**

There is no setup fee, no contract length, and no early termination penalty. 30-day notice to cancel is to give the system time to wind down cleanly, not to lock you in.

### 5.5 Section — How it works

**Section header:**
> How onboarding goes.

Five-step list:

**Step 1 — Tier 1 fit call (45 minutes).**
We confirm Tier 1 is right for your operation. Match volume expectations, criteria refinement, exclusivity window mechanics. Some carriers walk away from this call and start on Tier 2 instead; that's fine. Tier 1 is for carriers who specifically want first-look on matched drivers.

**Step 2 — Contract and onboarding.**
Standard 1-page Tier 1 agreement, then we capture hiring criteria in detail. Tier 1 criteria intake is more thorough than Tier 2 because you're paying for precision — we spend more time getting it right.

**Step 3 — Tenstreet integration setup.**
Same as Tier 2 — we configure the lead delivery integration with your Tenstreet account. Typically one business week.

**Step 4 — Test lead and dry-run.**
Synthetic test lead through the integration, plus a dry-run of the exclusivity window mechanics so you see exactly how the 24-hour first-look works.

**Step 5 — You're live.**
Matched drivers start landing in your exclusivity window the same day. Your first quarterly business review is scheduled for 90 days out.

### 5.6 FAQ

**Section header:**
> Common questions.

**How does the 24-hour exclusivity window actually work?**
When a driver completes intake and matches your criteria, their prequalification record is held back from Tier 2 carriers for 24 hours. You see it first. If you pursue, you submit through your normal Tenstreet workflow. If you don't pursue within 24 hours, the driver becomes visible to Tier 2 carriers in their match list. The driver is never told they were in an exclusivity window — from their side, your carrier just appeared in their matches.

**What happens if multiple Tier 1 carriers match the same driver?**
The driver's match list shows all Tier 1 carriers who matched, ranked by match-fit score against the driver's preferences. The 24-hour window applies equally to all Tier 1 matches — the first Tier 1 carrier to engage isn't given preference over others.

**Can we negotiate the $2,500 rate?**
No. The flat rate is part of the model. We don't do volume discounts, contract length discounts, or promotional pricing. Carriers paying the same rate eliminates a class of conversation that doesn't help anyone — the rate is the rate.

**Is there a contract length?**
No. Month-to-month with 30-day notice. The contract is one page and exists mostly to document the commercial terms in writing. We're not interested in locking in carriers who don't want to be there.

**What if Tier 2 carriers see the same driver after the window?**
After 24 hours, the driver's match list shows both Tier 1 and Tier 2 matches. Drivers pick which carriers they want to release their prequalification to. A Tier 2 carrier who appears in a driver's match list after the Tier 1 window may still get the lead — the driver chooses. The exclusivity window is a head start, not an exclusive right.

**Do we have to use Tenstreet?**
The free integration is for Tenstreet specifically. Tier 1 carriers on other ATS platforms can still subscribe — leads are delivered by email instead of integrated to the ATS. The 24-hour exclusivity window applies either way.

**How do we cancel?**
Email or call your account contact. 30 days from the notice date, the subscription ends and your carrier moves to Tier 2 (or out entirely if you prefer). No penalty, no exit fee, no signed paperwork.

**What does the quarterly business review actually cover?**
Match volume against your criteria, your conversion data (how many matches led to applications, how many led to hires), criteria refinement based on what's working and what isn't, and any product changes or new features. It's a working session, not a sales call.

## 7. Design notes

### 7.1 Visual differentiation from driver pages

Carrier pages should feel distinct from driver-facing surfaces:

- Tighter, more deliberate layout. Less white space than the driver homepage.
- More density on pricing tables and feature lists. Recruiting directors want scannable detail.
- Color palette uses CDLA.jobs deep blue (`#1F3A5F`) and medium blue (`#2E5C8A`) more heavily. Gold accent (`#D4A017`) is used sparingly — for CTAs and pricing emphasis only.
- No chatbox. No mention of Debbie. Carrier pages are not driver pages.
- No stock photos of trucks or generic businesspeople. Brand-spare design or no imagery at all.

### 7.2 Typography

Same Inter font family as the rest of the platform. Body copy slightly tighter than driver pages (carriers can handle denser text).

### 7.3 Pricing table emphasis

The pricing transparency tables on both pages should be visually prominent — not buried mid-page. Recruiting directors specifically look for "what does this actually cost" and the table is the answer. Worth designing as a callout box, not a plain table.

### 7.4 No emojis (per Brand Voice Guide)

Same rule as everywhere else. No emojis in copy, no emoji icons in feature lists.

## 8. Voice guidelines

Carrier-facing voice per Brand Voice Guide §4:

- Professional and credible
- Specific over vague — real prices, real timelines, real specifics
- Acknowledge complexity rather than smooth over it (FCRA, DOT 391, prequalification mechanics)
- Quote regulations precisely when relevant ("49 CFR 391.23" not "the background check rule")
- No corporate buzzwords: synergy, scalable, mission-critical, end-to-end, holistic, omnichannel
- No vague differentiators ("we're different because we care")
- No high-pressure language ("limited time," "lock in your rate")

Examples of on-voice phrasing in this spec:

- "We're a matching service, not a recruiter."
- "Tier 1 is for carriers who specifically want first-look on matched drivers."
- "$2,500 per month is flat regardless of match volume."
- "We don't replace your ATS. We feed it pre-qualified candidates."

Examples of off-voice phrasing to avoid:

- "Unlock unprecedented access to high-quality CDL-A talent"
- "Transform your driver hiring with our industry-leading platform"
- "Schedule a free no-obligation consultation today!"
- "Limited Tier 1 slots available — secure yours now"

## 9. Open questions

### 9.1 Calendar booking integration

Both pages reference "Schedule a call" CTAs. Open: which calendar tool (Calendly, Cal.com, GHL's built-in scheduler, etc.) and what calendar does it route to (Todd's directly, a sales team queue, a shared inbox)? Decision needed before launch.

### 9.2 PDF download mechanics

"Download the carrier brief (PDF)" references the existing Carrier Pitch Deck Outline v1 (currently a .docx). Open:
- Is the PDF version a direct export of the deck, or a separate one-pager?
- Is the download gated behind an email submission (lead capture) or open?

I'd recommend ungated download — gating it costs more in friction than the email address is worth at the carrier stage. But it's a defensible call to gate for lead tracking.

### 9.3 Tier 1 trial offer

The Carrier Pitch Deck Outline §5.2 mentions a possible "we'll prorate the first month if you start before the 15th" trial. Should this appear on the exclusivity page, or stay as a sales-rep-only offer?

I'd recommend keeping it sales-rep-only. Promotional pricing on the landing page contradicts the "flat rate is the rate" framing.

### 9.4 Carrier testimonials

Same as homepage — no real testimonials exist yet. Add a testimonial section to both pages in v2 once real ones are available. For v1, no testimonial section at all (better than fake or generic ones).

### 9.5 Case studies

Carrier pitch deck reserves Slide 11 for case studies, marked as placeholder until Phase 4-6 of the build. Landing pages should follow the same pattern — no case studies in v1; add in v2 with real data and partner consent.

### 9.6 Cross-tier navigation

When a carrier lands on `/partners/integration` and decides they actually want Tier 1, how do they navigate?
- A small "Considering exclusivity? See Tier 1 →" link in the FAQ or footer of the integration page
- Same in reverse on the exclusivity page
- No cross-navigation; carrier asks sales

I'd recommend small in-page cross-navigation links. Doesn't dilute the focused pitch but doesn't strand carriers either.
