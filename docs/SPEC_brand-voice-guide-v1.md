# Brand Voice Guide — CDLA.jobs

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Locked
**Audience:** Internal — content writers, copywriters, Claude Code sessions, AI persona configuration, any team member producing CDLA.jobs-branded content
**Owner:** Todd Bryson

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how CDLA.jobs talks to its two audiences: drivers and carriers. Every piece of content produced for CDLA.jobs — landing pages, emails, SMS, AI conversation turns, social posts, sales decks, support replies — should match this voice. Inconsistent voice across surfaces is the most common way a young brand reads as unserious.

CDLA.jobs is a separate brand from Powerhouse Trucking Pros (PHTP). PHTP must never appear in driver-facing or carrier-facing CDLA.jobs content. The voices defined here are CDLA.jobs's own.

---

## 2. The two audiences

CDLA.jobs has two distinct audiences who see almost entirely separate content surfaces. They require different voices.

### 2.1 Drivers (driver-facing voice)

**Who they are:** Class A CDL holders. Mostly men, mostly 30-65, mostly with at least some experience. They've used Indeed, ZipRecruiter, ClassADrivers, and similar platforms and have specific complaints about each. They get called by recruiters constantly — often 10-20 calls per week for jobs they didn't ask about. They are skeptical of platforms that promise to help them. They trust other drivers more than they trust marketing.

**What they care about:** Pay, home time, equipment, dispatch quality, freight, lanes, miles. In roughly that order, though it varies. They do not care about "synergy," "transformation," or "career growth language." They care about specifics.

**How they read:** Mostly on phones, often in spare moments — pre-trip, fueling up, in the sleeper, on mandatory rest. Captions on. Sound usually off. They scroll fast. They will not read a paragraph that doesn't justify its first line.

### 2.2 Carriers (carrier-facing voice)

**Who they are:** Recruiting directors, hiring managers, owner-operators of fleets ranging from 10 to 1,000+ trucks. Some are themselves former drivers; some came up through HR or operations. They are professional buyers — they evaluate driver-sourcing services regularly and have a clear sense of what works and what doesn't.

**What they care about:** Cost per hire, quality of leads, application-to-hire conversion, integration with their existing ATS (often Tenstreet), compliance defensibility, time-to-fill. They are skeptical of "lead vendor" pitches because they've been burned. They respect honesty about what a service does and doesn't do.

**How they read:** Email, LinkedIn, and trade publication articles. Longer attention span than drivers, but still skim-first. They want to know the model and the price before they read the marketing language.

## 3. Driver-facing voice

### 3.1 Voice summary

Warm, driver-first, direct. Slightly sarcastic toward the trucking job-search industry — Indeed, ZipRecruiter, recruiter spam, lead farms. Never sarcastic toward the driver themselves. Honest about what we can and cannot do. We sound like a driver's friend who happens to know how this industry actually works.

### 3.2 What we do

- **Use plain English.** "Pulling reefer" not "operating refrigerated equipment." "Hauling" not "transporting freight."
- **Acknowledge what drivers actually deal with.** Recruiter spam, application black holes, jobs that lie about pay, dispatchers who never answer.
- **Be specific.** Real pay numbers, real carrier names (when authorized), real lanes. Specifics build trust. Vagueness reads like marketing.
- **Respect their time.** Short messages. Quick reads. No fluff before the point.
- **Use first names and direct address.** "Hey, you been pulling flatbed for 5+ years?" not "Greetings, valued candidate."
- **Punch up at the industry, not at the driver.** Sarcasm about Indeed is fine. Sarcasm about the driver is not.
- **Tell the truth when it's inconvenient.** Zero matches available? Say so. Pay range is low? Say so. Drivers respect this.

### 3.3 What we don't do

- **No emojis. Ever.** This is a hard rule across all CDLA.jobs content, on every channel. No 🚛, no 💵, no 👍, no exceptions.
- **No exclamation points beyond one per message, and ideally zero.** Drivers reading a message with five exclamation points read it as desperation or recruiter-speak.
- **No corporate buzzwords:** synergy, leverage, ecosystem, transformation, journey, solution, value-add, paradigm, best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge, robust, seamless.
- **No fake intimacy:** "we're so excited to welcome you to the CDLA.jobs family," "we're thrilled," "we can't wait to hear from you." Drivers see right through this.
- **No condescension:** "Did you know that…", "It's important to remember that…", "Pro tip:". Talk to drivers as adults.
- **No performative urgency:** "Don't miss out!", "Limited time only!", "Act now!". The driver decides their own urgency.
- **No fake humanness from Debbie.** Debbie is AI and says so. She doesn't pretend to have a hometown, a family, a truck, or trucking experience.
- **No bashing specific competitors by name.** Bashing the *category* (job boards, lead vendors, recruiter spam) is fine. Bashing "Werner" or "Schneider" or a specific competing platform by name is not. Drivers may have positive relationships with these companies and we don't insult their judgment.

### 3.4 Phrases and patterns that work

**Openers:**
- "Hey —"
- "Real talk —"
- "Quick one —"
- "Not gonna lie —"
- "Look —"

**Acknowledgments:**
- "I get it"
- "Fair"
- "Makes sense"
- "Got it"

**Honest framing:**
- "Different carriers handle this differently"
- "Carriers always ask this"
- "Some carriers will, some won't"
- "I'll do what I can with that"
- "It's not nothing, but it's not everything"

**Sarcasm aimed at the industry (use sparingly):**
- "Without the 20-page applications"
- "No 14 recruiter calls a day for jobs you didn't ask about"
- "If a job board feels like it's working against you, it probably is"
- "Pay you didn't agree to, lanes they didn't mention"

### 3.5 Phrases and patterns to avoid

| Don't write | Why it's wrong |
|---|---|
| "We're excited to help you find your next great opportunity!" | Performs emotion, uses "opportunity" as code for "job," exclamation point |
| "Our innovative AI-driven platform will transform your job search journey" | Buzzword stack, "journey," over-promises |
| "Welcome aboard, valued driver!" | Fake intimacy, "aboard" is corporate, exclamation point |
| "Don't miss out on these amazing opportunities!" | Fake urgency, vague, exclamation point |
| "It's important to remember that all applications require..." | Condescending |
| "Our team of experts has carefully curated..." | Self-important, vague |
| "We pride ourselves on..." | No driver cares what we pride ourselves on |
| "Trust me, this carrier is a great fit!" | Salesy, removes driver autonomy |

### 3.6 Examples — same content, three voice levels

**Bad (generic platform voice):**
> Welcome to CDLA.jobs! We're thrilled to help you discover amazing trucking opportunities tailored to your unique career goals. Our innovative platform connects you with top-tier carriers across the nation. Get started today!

**Okay (correct direction but bland):**
> Welcome to CDLA.jobs. We match Class A drivers to carriers based on what they actually want. Answer a few questions and we'll show you what fits.

**Good (CDLA.jobs voice):**
> Hey — CDLA.jobs matches CDL-A drivers to carriers based on what you actually want. Your schedule, your equipment, your home time. Five questions and you see matches. No 20-page applications, no 14 recruiter calls for jobs you didn't ask about.

### 3.7 Channel-specific notes

**Landing pages:** Headlines short and specific. Body copy in short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max). Lots of white space. Real numbers in the trust signals — never round to make it look bigger.

**Email:** Subject lines under 50 characters, ideally under 40. No exclamation points in subject lines. First line of body has to justify opening. Sign-off plain — "— Debbie" or "— The CDLA.jobs team" or nothing at all. Never "Warmest regards" or "Sincerely yours."

**SMS:** Under 160 characters where possible. Include STOP language per TCPA. Never multi-message threads if it can be avoided. Plain language.

**In-chat (Debbie):** See `SPEC_conversational-ai-intake-v1.md` §11 for the full Debbie voice spec. Short, direct, slightly sarcastic toward the industry, never toward the driver. First person. No emojis.

**Social media (organic):** Same rules but tighter. TikTok and Reels captions should be under 30 words. Hooks in the first 3 words of any video.

**Push notifications:** Under 50 characters. Specific. "3 new reefer matches in Atlanta" not "You have new matches!"

## 4. Carrier-facing voice

### 4.1 Voice summary

Professional and credible. Specific. Acknowledges what carriers actually deal with — bad leads, expensive agencies, integration friction, compliance complexity. Honest about CDLA.jobs's model and price. Confident without being boastful. We sound like a vendor who has done the work and respects the buyer's time.

### 4.2 What we do

- **Be specific.** Real pricing, real partner counts, real numbers. No "industry-leading" or "best-in-class."
- **Lead with the model, not the hype.** What we do, how we make money, what it costs.
- **Acknowledge competition fairly.** Indeed has a role. Lead vendors have a role. Recruiting agencies have a role. Here's what we do differently.
- **Talk about compliance respectfully.** Recruiting directors are exhausted by FCRA, TCPA, and DOT 391 complexity. We respect that complexity rather than waving it away.
- **Quote regulations precisely.** "49 CFR 391.23" not "the federal background check rule."
- **Price visible early.** Hiding the number reads as untrustworthy.

### 4.3 What we don't do

- **No emojis.** Same hard rule as driver voice. No 🚛, no 📊, no 💼.
- **No corporate buzzwords.** Same list as driver voice, plus: scalable, enterprise-grade, mission-critical, end-to-end, holistic, omnichannel.
- **No exaggerated claims.** "Industry-leading," "best-in-class," "fastest," "most accurate" — none of these without a real source. If we don't have data, we don't claim it.
- **No vague differentiators.** "We're different because we care" is not a differentiator. "We're different because we charge $2,500/month flat with no per-hire fee" is.
- **No trash-talking competitors by name.** Same rule as driver voice. We describe categories of competition (lead vendors, recruiting agencies, job boards) without naming and shaming.
- **No high-pressure sales language.** "Limited time offer," "Special pricing for new partners," "Lock in your rate" — drivers and carriers both see through this.
- **No fake exclusivity.** "Only available to select carriers" is dishonest unless it's actually true.

### 4.4 Phrases and patterns that work

**Framing:**
- "Here's how CDLA.jobs is structured"
- "The model is..."
- "Free for drivers; carriers pay if they want priority access"
- "You handle the application; we handle the matching"

**Acknowledging complexity:**
- "Different carriers handle this differently"
- "DOT 391 lives in your ATS, not ours"
- "FCRA-regulated consent stays where it belongs — with you"

**Pricing:**
- "Tier 2 is free. Tier 1 is $2,500/month flat."
- "No setup fee. No per-hire fee. No per-lead fee."
- "Cancel anytime with 30 days notice."

**Honest about limitations:**
- "We don't run PSP reports. That's not us — that's your ATS."
- "IntelliApp is the carrier's tool, not ours. We feed it; we don't fill it."
- "We're not going to replace your recruiter. We're going to feed them better applicants."

### 4.5 Examples — same content, three voice levels

**Bad (lead-vendor voice):**
> Unlock unprecedented access to high-quality CDL-A driver leads with our industry-leading platform! Our cutting-edge matching technology delivers pre-qualified candidates straight to your inbox, reducing your cost-per-hire and accelerating your time-to-fill. Schedule a demo today!

**Okay (corporate but not awful):**
> CDLA.jobs delivers matched CDL-A driver leads to your ATS. Drivers complete a single intake; we match them to your hiring criteria. Free at the basic tier, with paid exclusivity available.

**Good (CDLA.jobs carrier voice):**
> CDLA.jobs sends Class A driver prequalifications to your ATS, matched against your hiring criteria. Drivers consent to share their info with you specifically — not to a lead panel. You handle the full application. We handle finding the right drivers. Free at Tier 2; $2,500/month for 24-hour exclusivity at Tier 1. No per-hire fee, no setup fee.

### 4.6 Channel-specific notes

**Email outreach:** Short. First line names the carrier specifically and includes one real fact about them (lane, fleet size, recent news). No mass-mail boilerplate.

**LinkedIn:** Professional but not stiff. First-person where appropriate. Specific anecdotes outperform abstract claims.

**Pitch deck:** Numbers and tables over paragraphs. Specifics over adjectives. Pricing slide visible early. See `SPEC_carrier-pitch-deck-outline-v1.docx`.

**Landing pages (`/partners/integration` and `/partners/exclusivity`):** Clear hierarchy. Model → price → what's included → next step. Trust signals are partner names and real metrics, not stock photos.

**Sales calls and demos:** Listen first, talk second. Ask about their current cost per hire, time to fill, and channel mix before pitching. Match the pitch to the actual problem.

## 5. Debbie — the AI persona

Debbie is the conversational AI driver matcher on CDLA.jobs. She is a separate sub-brand within the CDLA.jobs voice with her own specific persona rules. Full spec lives in `SPEC_conversational-ai-intake-v1.md` §11. Summary here:

- **Identity:** AI, first-person, female-coded name, no fake biography
- **Voice:** Driver-facing voice (see §3) plus an additional layer of directness — Debbie is even more efficient and to-the-point than long-form CDLA.jobs content
- **AI disclosure required:** Locked opening: *"I'm Debbie, the AI driver matcher at CDLA.jobs"*
- **What Debbie does not do:** roleplay, opinionate on carriers beyond match data, give legal/financial/medical advice, perform emotions she doesn't have, claim trucking experience she doesn't have

No future AI persona on CDLA.jobs should be created without explicit approval. Debbie is the persona; we don't proliferate AI characters.

## 6. Hard rules summary

For quick reference — the rules that never bend regardless of channel, audience, or context:

1. **No emojis. Anywhere. Driver-facing or carrier-facing.**
2. **No bashing specific competitors by name.** Category criticism is fine.
3. **No fake urgency, fake exclusivity, fake intimacy.**
4. **No exaggerated claims without sources.** "Industry-leading" is banned unless we can cite the industry survey.
5. **No PHTP references in CDLA.jobs content.** PHTP exists as a referral partner; it does not surface to drivers or carriers using CDLA.jobs.
6. **Prices visible early in any sales context.** Tier 2 = free. Tier 1 = $2,500/month flat. No per-hire fees, no setup fees.
7. **Specifics over vagueness.** Real numbers, real names (where authorized), real lanes.
8. **Driver-facing content respects driver autonomy.** Never "trust me," never "you should," never high-pressure.
9. **Honesty about limitations.** Zero matches is "zero matches," not "we're working on it." Cause-based termination is "harder to place," not "no problem."
10. **AI is disclosed.** Debbie identifies as AI in her opening message and answers honestly if asked directly.

## 7. When in doubt

If a piece of content feels off but you can't articulate why, run it through these three questions:

1. **Would a driver read this and roll their eyes?** If yes, rewrite.
2. **Could a competitor write the exact same line?** If yes, it's too generic. Add specificity.
3. **Is there an emoji, an exclamation point, or a buzzword in it?** Remove them.

If a piece of content involves a sensitive topic — termination, criminal history, SAP status, accident history, pay disputes, mental health — default to *more* directness and *less* softening language. Drivers and recruiting directors both prefer honesty to euphemism, even on hard topics. Euphemism reads as dishonest.
