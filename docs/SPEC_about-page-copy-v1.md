**Version:** 1.0

**Audience:** Internal — product, engineering, content writers, designers

---

## 1. Purpose

This document specifies the copy for the CDLA.jobs About page at `cdla.jobs/about`. The About page is linked from the homepage footer and from secondary navigation. It is not a primary conversion surface — its job is to provide trust and context for visitors (drivers, carriers, journalists, partners) who want to know who built the platform and why.

The About page does not reference Powerhouse Trucking Pros (PHTP). The brand wall between PHTP and CDLA.jobs is maintained on the marketing surface. The PHTP referral relationship is disclosed in the privacy policy and Stage 2 consent language where the law requires it, not on this page.

---

## 2. Strategic context

The About page is a credibility document, not a sales document. Most drivers will never visit it. The drivers who do are typically more skeptical, more experienced, or more deliberate than the average user — they want to know who's behind the platform before they hand over their CDL information. The page must be honest, specific, and short enough to read in two minutes.

The page is driver-facing in voice but accessible to carriers and other parties. Same brand voice as the homepage: warm, driver-first, direct, no fake humility, no buzzwords, no emojis.

---

## 3. Page structure

Sections render top to bottom:

1. Mission statement
2. The problem (what's broken with the current model)
3. What CDLA.jobs does
4. Who built it (Todd's founder section)
5. What we believe (operating principles)
6. Contact / next step

Target length: 400-600 words of body copy. Tight enough to read quickly, long enough to feel like a real company page rather than a placeholder.

---

## 4. Section 1 — Mission statement

Top of the page, set in large type. Single line:

> CDLA.jobs exists to give CDL-A drivers a hiring process that respects their time.

No subhead. No supporting paragraph. The line stands on its own. The rest of the page expands on what that means in practice.

---

## 5. Section 2 — The problem

### 5.1 Section header

> Hiring is broken on both sides.

### 5.2 Body copy

The way CDL-A drivers find jobs hasn't really changed in 20 years. Apply to a dozen carriers. Fill out the same information a dozen times. Wait for recruiters to call back — except half of them never do, and the other half are pitching jobs you didn't ask about, for equipment you don't run, in regions you don't want.

The carriers see the same broken thing from the other side. Their recruiters spend half their day on applications that were never going to qualify. Cost per hire keeps climbing. Time to fill drags out. Everyone loses except the job boards that get paid whether or not anyone gets hired.

### 5.3 Voice notes

Direct, specific. "Half of them never call back" is the kind of specific number that lands harder than "a poor response rate." The driver-facing voice rule is "specific over vague" and this section leans on it.

No competitor names in this section. The Indeed mention is held back for the founder section, where it lands as personal motivation rather than generic complaint.

---

## 6. Section 3 — What CDLA.jobs does

### 6.1 Section header

> A different model.

### 6.2 Body copy

CDLA.jobs is an AI-driven matching platform. Drivers tell us once what they want — their experience, their equipment, their schedule preference, the kind of carrier they're looking for. We match them to carriers actually hiring drivers like them. The driver picks which carriers get their information. Nobody else does.

No 20-page applications until the driver is ready to apply to a specific carrier. No selling driver contact info to lead panels. No bombarding drivers with calls about jobs they didn't ask about. Drivers stay in control of who sees their information and when.

We don't run background checks. We don't pull MVRs. We don't replace the carrier's hiring process. We feed it better-qualified candidates so everyone's time gets spent on conversations that might actually lead somewhere.

### 6.3 Voice notes

This section mirrors language from the homepage but expands the detail slightly. The "we don't" list at the end is intentional — it tells the carrier-side reader exactly where the platform's responsibilities end, which is reassuring for any recruiting director who's read about FCRA-related liability and wonders if CDLA.jobs is going to drag them into something.

## 7. Section 4 — Who built it

### 7.1 Section header

> Built by Todd Bryson.

### 7.2 Body copy

Todd has been designing software since 2015 and working in truck driver recruiting since 2021. Along the way he's built, bought, and sold four companies — two acquisitions and two exits — most of them at the intersection of trucking and technology.

CDLA.jobs is the platform he wishes had existed back when he started in this space. He spent enough years watching drivers waste their time on Ind**d (and watching recruiters waste theirs sorting through the resulting noise) to know exactly what the broken parts of the model are. CDLA.jobs is the unbroken version.

### 7.3 Voice notes

- The "Ind**d" stylization is the agreed compromise on the brand voice rule. Used exactly once, on this page, in the founder section. Reads as personal frustration rather than competitive marketing. Not used anywhere else on the platform.
- "Four companies — two acquisitions and two exits" is more specific and credible than "several companies." Specificity builds trust.
- "The platform he wishes had existed" is the standard founder-origin framing. It's a cliché but it works because it's honest.
- The bio is short on purpose. No "graduated from X University." No "named to the Forbes 30 Under 30." No credentialing. The brand voice rules out that kind of self-importance.

### 7.4 Photo

A real photo of Todd would belong here if available. Optional for v1 launch. If included:
- Real photo, not stock
- Casual professional (not suit-and-tie, not selfie-with-truck)
- Black-and-white or color, designer's call
- Sized small (200x200 or similar) — this is a founder bio, not a billboard

If not included, the section reads fine without a photo.

## 8. Section 5 — What we believe

### 8.1 Section header

> What we believe.

### 8.2 Format

Four short statements, each a sentence or two. Display as a stacked list, not a grid. No icons.

### 8.3 The four statements

**Drivers run their own search.**
The driver decides which carriers see their information. We don't sell it. We don't share it with anyone they didn't pick.

**Carriers pay if they want to. Drivers always pay zero.**
The platform is free for drivers. Carriers can subscribe for priority access if they want it. Drivers never pay us anything, ever.

**Honest matching, even when it's inconvenient.**
If a driver's profile makes them hard to place, we say so. If we don't have matches in their region, we say so. We don't pretend.

**The hiring decision stays with the carrier.**
We're a matching service, not a recruiter. We don't promise anyone they'll be hired. We connect drivers to carriers and step out of the way.

### 8.4 Voice notes

- "Even when it's inconvenient" is the most distinctive line on the page. It's a direct response to the platform pattern of pretending zero matches exist or hiding hard truths from drivers. On-brand.
- "We don't pretend" mirrors language used on the homepage ("we don't pad the results"). Repetition of this idea across surfaces is intentional — it's a core brand position.

## 11. Open questions

### 11.1 Founder photo

Optional for v1. If used, requirements are in Section 7.4. Decision: include in v1 launch or add later?

### 11.2 Email addresses

Section 9 references three email patterns (drivers@, partners@, press@). Need to confirm:
- Which email addresses will be real and monitored at launch
- Where they route (Todd directly, shared inbox, ticketing system)
- Whether a single email is preferable for simplicity at launch

### 11.3 The bought/sold companies — should they be named?

Section 7 says "four companies — two acquisitions and two exits" without naming them. Naming them adds credibility but also surfaces information that could be researched further (including the PHTP connection if any of the four companies are linked to PHTP in public records).

Recommendation: don't name them in v1. The general claim is enough. Name them later if there's a strategic reason to.

### 11.4 LinkedIn or social links for Todd

Some About pages link to the founder's LinkedIn. Decision: include a link, or keep the page self-contained? If included, the LinkedIn profile should be consistent with the About page bio (especially regarding PHTP).

### 11.5 Update cadence

The About page tends to get written once and never touched. When does it need updating?
- After v1 launch + 90 days: refresh based on what's actually true (real carrier count, real driver numbers, any press)
- Founder bio updated when any new credentials apply
- Mission/beliefs section locked until a brand pivot, if ever
